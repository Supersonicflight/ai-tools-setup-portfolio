Date: 2026

Key Insights:

Key Insights:

- Embrace the framework: Master product development by starting with what is proven, improving it to make it better, and then introducing what is new.
- Copying is moral arbitrage: Ethically cloning a highly validated human behavior minimizes unnecessary market risk and reduces industrial waste. 
- Earn the right to innovate: Secure predictable retention metrics first by relying on established mechanics before introducing risky user flows.
- Build a failure machine: Construct low-cost validation systems to ruthlessly test demand signals before writing any scalable production code. 
- Kill hope early: Protect your organization's trajectory by abandoning failing expressions of an idea while preserving the core underlying consumer instinct.

Title:
Linear’s secret to building beloved B2B products | Nan Yu (Head of Product)

Date:
2025

Link:

Key Insights:

- Competence replaces quality trade-offs: Speed and quality are not oppositional forces; true competence allows expert builders to rapidly create high-quality, iterative output.
- Prioritize users over reporting managers: Reject custom reporting fields requested by middle management if those additions increase friction for individual contributors.
- Map features to specific humans: Build conviction for new tools by tracing demands directly to real, individual customer workflows rather than generalized personas.
- Iterate using extreme product attributes: Validate core functionality early by shipping polarizing, extreme variations of features internally before finding a balanced compromise.
- Treat deadlines as p0 requirements: Treat launch dates as absolute p0 priorities by maintaining few deadlines and aggressively cutting scope to hit them.
