Title:
Mastering Messaging for B2B Tech with Emma Stratton

Date:
2026

Link:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8YFy06-eRM

Key Insights:

- Define the distinct roles: Positioning dictates the core strategy, messaging shapes that strategy into words, and copywriting refines those words for channel conversion.

- Make it punchy: Strip out bloated corporate jargon and long sentences to express your value with raw power and fewer words.

- Use simple verbs: Ditch overused tech terms like accelerate or leverage for clear, human words like speed up or use.

- Target by awareness level: Narrow your focus onto your customer's specific mindset and previous tool experience to dictate how heavily you teach or differentiate.

- Pitch specific superpowers: Avoid generic three-word taglines and write headlines that explicitly show the clear, measurable future capability the customer gains.

Key Insights:

- Market shifts require repositioning: When massive changes alter user behavior, your product's positioning must evolve to match the new market reality.

- Focus on immediate relevance: In a turbulent or shifting landscape, highlight the specific capabilities that solve your buyer's most urgent, pressing problem right now.

- Ditch outdated comparisons: Do not position yourself against old-school market frameworks that your customers are actively moving away from.

- Educate on the new way: Use your content to clearly explain how the market has fundamentally changed and what it means for the buyer's business model.

- Differentiate through conviction: Take a clear, unmistakable stance on where the industry is heading to earn deep strategic trust from prospects.

Title:
New Thinking on Market Categories

Date:
2026

Link:
https://www.positioning.show/new-thinking-on-market-categories/

Key Insights:

-Aspirational categories cause confusion: Choosing an overly ambitious market category creates deep confusion and weakens your core positioning.

- Categories are understanding tools: A market category's true job is to guide immediate customer comprehension, not to replace clear messaging.

- The wrong category backfires: Selecting an incorrect category triggers false customer assumptions that your sales team must constantly fight to undo.

- Align categories with unique value: Ensure your chosen market category naturally highlights and supports your specific, differentiated product capabilities.

- New categories carry high risks: Creating a brand-new market category demands immense resources and comes with long-term strategic challenges.
